## PART 1: CORE CONCEPTS CHEAT SHEET

### What is TOGAF?
| **Aspect** | **Definition** |
|------------|----------------|
| **Full Name** | The Open Group Architecture Framework |
| **Type** | Architecture framework for Enterprise Architecture |
| **Components** | Methods, tools, and common definitions |
| **Primary Purpose** | Provide common language for stakeholders, coworkers, and business associates |

---

### What is an Architecture Framework?
> "A framework is how we do architecture. It's the guideline for creating the design of your enterprise."

---

## PART 2: ENTERPRISE ARCHITECTURE DEFINITIONS

### ISO/IEC/IEEE 42010 Definition
> "The architecture are the fundamental concepts or properties of a system in its environment, embodied in its elements, relationships and in the principles of its design and evolution."

**Key Elements:**
- Fundamental concepts/properties
- Elements of the system
- Relationships between elements
- Principles of design and evolution

---

### TOGAF Definition
> "The structure of components, their interrelationships and the principles and guidelines governing their design and evolution over time."

**The THREE Core Elements:**
1. **Components** - The parts of the system
2. **Relationships** - How components interact
3. **Principles** - Guidelines governing design and evolution

---

### Comparison Table: ISO vs TOGAF Definitions

| **ISO/IEC/IEEE 42010** | **TOGAF** |
|------------------------|-----------|
| Fundamental concepts/properties | Structure of components |
| Elements of the system | Their interrelationships |
| Relationships between elements | Principles and guidelines |
| Principles of design and evolution | Governing design and evolution over time |

---

## PART 3: THE ARCHITECTURE DEVELOPMENT METHOD (ADM)

### Key Point
> **"Central to the TOGAF framework is the Architecture Development Method (ADM)."**

**The ADM is:**
- A **method** contained within TOGAF
- The core methodology for developing enterprise architecture
- One of the "methods" in the framework (along with tools and definitions)

---

### Why Principles Matter

> *"Once you've defined a set of architecture principles, then in the following six months, in the following six years, when a question comes to you, 'Hey, we would like to do this, how are we going to do it?' you already have some of the fundamental answers."*

**Purpose of Principles:**
- Answer questions about approach
- Guide decision-making
- Provide consistency over time
- Establish "do we do this or do we not?"
- Define "how do we approach solving these problems?"

### True/False Questions

**9. TOGAF is only for IT architecture.**
- **False** - It's for enterprise architecture (whole organization)

**10. The Architecture Development Method is not part of TOGAF.**
- **False** - It's central to the framework

**11. Architecture principles help guide decisions over time.**
- **True**

**12. The ISO definition and TOGAF definition of architecture are completely different.**
- **False** - They essentially say the same thing

---

### Short Answer Questions

**13. What are the three elements that make up an architecture according to TOGAF?**

> **Answer:** Components, interrelationships, and principles/guidelines.

**14. Why is a common language important in enterprise architecture?**

> **Answer:** A common language ensures that everyone (coworkers, stakeholders, business associates) is on the same page when communicating about the architecture.

**15. Explain the difference between an architecture framework and an architecture.**

> **Answer:** An architecture framework is the guideline for how to do architecture (the method, tools, and definitions). An architecture is the actual design - the components, relationships, and principles of the system.

**16. What is the purpose of architecture principles?**

> **Answer:** Architecture principles provide fundamental answers about how to approach decisions. They guide "do we do this or not?" and "how do we approach solving these problems?" over the long term.

**17. How would you describe the relationship between ADM and TOGAF?**

> **Answer:** The ADM is a method contained within TOGAF. TOGAF is the overall framework containing methods (including ADM), tools, and common definitions.

---

### Essay/Discussion Questions

**18. Compare and contrast the ISO/IEC/IEEE 42010 definition of architecture with the TOGAF definition. What are the similarities and differences?**

> **Guide:** Both define architecture as involving elements/components, relationships, and principles. The ISO definition emphasizes "fundamental concepts" and "properties in its environment," while TOGAF uses "structure of components" and "guidelines." Both address evolution over time.

**19. Explain why architecture principles are important for long-term organizational success. Use the transcript content to support your answer.**

> **Guide:** Principles provide consistency over time, help answer questions years later, guide decision-making, and establish fundamental approaches that don't require re-debating.

**20. Using the building architecture analogy, explain what enterprise architecture is.**

> **Guide:** Like a building's architecture includes drawings, relationships between elements, and principles of design - enterprise architecture similarly includes the components of a system, their relationships, and guiding principles.

## PART 5: KEY TERMS FLASHCARD

| **Term** | **Definition** |
|----------|----------------|
| **TOGAF** | The Open Group Architecture Framework |
| **Architecture Framework** | A set of methods, tools, and common definitions; how we do architecture |
| **Enterprise Architecture** | The components, relationships, and principles of an enterprise system |
| **ADM** | Architecture Development Method - central method in TOGAF |
| **Architecture Principles** | Guidelines governing design and evolution over time |
| **Components** | The elements of a system/architecture |
| **Interrelationships** | How components relate to each other |

---

## PART 6: EXAM TIPS

### Remember These Key Points:

1. **TOGAF = Framework (methods + tools + definitions)**
2. **ADM = Method (contained within TOGAF)**
3. **Architecture = Components + Relationships + Principles**
4. **Principles = Guidance for design and evolution over time**
5. **Common language = Ensures everyone is on same page**

### Common Exam Traps:
- Don't confuse "framework" with "architecture"
- Don't forget the three elements of architecture (components, relationships, principles)
- Remember: TOGAF and ISO definitions say essentially the same thing
- Architecture principles are for long-term guidance, not just IT
